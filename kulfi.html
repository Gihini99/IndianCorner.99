                                                                                                               Kulfi

Kulfi is an Indian ice cream made by boiling full-fat milk (and sometimes nuts and spices like cardamom or saffron) down until it’s thick and creamy. Mention it to any South Asian person, and their eyes will light up with joy and happy memories. Make it, and you'll see why!

Ingredients

2 3/4 c. - whole milk

2 c.  - heavy cream

1/4 tsp. - kosher salt 

1/2 tsp.  - ground cardamom

1/3 c.  - granulated sugar

1/3 c.  - roasted pistachios, finely chopped

Directions  

Add milk, heavy cream, salt, and cardamom into a heavy-bottom saucepan over medium-high heat. Stir continuously until the milk comes to a boil, about 6 minutes. Simmer on low heat for 45 minutes, stirring often. Make sure to keep an eye on the milk as it might overflow. 

Stir in sugar and simmer for an additional 6 minutes. Then, add the pistachios and simmer for 10 more minutes. Cool the mixture completely. The mixture should be thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.

Pour the liquid into ice pop molds and freeze for at least 5 hours. Set the kulfi out on the counter for 5 minutes before unmolding.

Use freshly ground cardamom—it’s more potent and the flavor will come through more. If you’re buying pre-ground cardamom, use an extra ¼ teaspoon.

- You can add whatever flavor you want. Saffron is common. Chocolate and fruit preserves would be delicious, too.
